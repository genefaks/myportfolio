User Centered Design


Research / Design / Prototyping / Testing / Evaluation

UL Buddies

UL Buddies is a platform that helps connect people of the same interests or similar interests. It gives a wholesome experience, especially to people who find it difficult to make friends. The application helps people to find people with common interests and bond over them or find a buddy to explore new interests.

Digital Prototype - Mobile App Screen

The application is driven with the idea of meeting people of common interests. We moved to the mock-up and designing low-fidelity prototype phase. A flow was created to guide users with navigating through the app. Users sign up and add their interests, then find people in the system who have same or similar interests and eventually meet people physically. Users were ranked with the number of similarities and range of interests the user has and display them when being listed.

Physical Prototype - Paper Prototype

A low-fidelity prototype was created using cardboard, brown paper, thin foam, glue, sticks and ropes as the material. This was used due to the accessibility of materials and easy changes during the user testing phase. The phone model was made with brown hard paper and white paper was cut to the shape creating the interactive screen for users. The white papers were glued together with a rope behind it to create a pull effect for the user when moving through screens giving the feel of how an application would naturally run on a phone.

Project Goal

Working in a team, create an interactive prototype using design tools and techniques to creatively explore the design space.

How it was accomplished

1

Define the Problem

We started off exploring areas of interest for each group member and came up with ideas that are impactful to people mentally, socially, physically and health-wise.

2

Brainstorming Ideas

Made use of creative tools such as, brainstorming, mind mapping, sketching and collaborating over group meetings, google meet calls till we came up with design solutions.

3

Narrowing Ideas

UL Buddies was picked based on the level of potential it had with solving a common problem we all faced. The idea around the development of the Buddies app is to help foster relationships.

4

User Research

Interacting with potential users to know their needs, problems they would like solved, platforms they use to solve the problem and the most important features on those platforms and why.

5

User Flow

Created a flow displaying the userâ€™s movement through the product, mapping out each step the user takes, from entry point right through to the final interaction.

6

Wireframing

Sketching out rough drafts using the user flow to show the page structure, layout and features of what each screen should look like before designing to high-fidelity screens.

7

Physical Prototype

Designing the low-fidelity prototype, we made a mockup phone using cardboard, a thin foam and glue. The mock-up phone is used to display the paper and run the paper through the screen to simulate using the app on a phone.

8

UI Design

Close to the final product, high fidelity prototypes were designed to be tested with. It contained most of the necessary design assets and components developed and integrated.

What I learnt

  • Working with the use of some design tools and techniques such as, brainstorming, mind mapping, sketching, collaboration to creatively explore the design space.
  • Brainstorming and Ideation to create a broad set of ideas.
  • Making use of paper prototypes to save costs and easy implementation of design feedbacks gotten from tests during early design stages.
